M.A.R.S — mars advanced recycling system

Three common non-metabolic wastes & how they can be repurposed for Martian construction.

Glass

Glass

Silicon-based, transparent, brittle

Glass on Mars (recycled bottles, panels, or shards) is mostly silicon dioxide derived. It is rigid and transparent, making it useful for light-transmitting panels or thermal glazing in habitat greenhouses.

What it can be
  • Translucent panels for greenhouse domes (light & partial insulation)
Key trait: transparency
•
Best combined with: regolith & binder
Metal

Metal

Strong, conductive, high tensile strength

Metal scraps (aluminum, steel, electronic frames) are valuable for load-bearing elements, reinforcements, and conductive traces. Metals can be melted or mechanically reworked into structural connectors or fibers.

What it can be
  • Fasteners, brackets, or reinforcement plates for habitats
Key trait: strength
•
Best combined with: melted plastics or regolith matrix
Plastic

Plastic

Thermoplastic waste: flexible & meltable

Common plastics (HDPE, PP, PET) can be shredded and melted; when mixed with regolith they form a durable composite that is easy to mold or extrude for bricks, tiles, or 3D printing filament.

What it can be
  • Molded bricks and tiles (plastic acts as binder)
Key trait: melt & bind
•
Best combined with: regolith powder (filler)
